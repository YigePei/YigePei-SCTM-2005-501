Museum Introduction

Aerial View of the Museum

The Dunhuang Museum was established in 1979 and completed in 2011. The architectural design was led by renowned Chinese architect Cui Kai, blending elements of the Great Wall beacons and ancient city fortresses. The museum spans 7,500 square meters and houses exhibition halls, artifact storage rooms, a screening hall, and a lounge area. The main exhibition theme is "A City of Cultural Fusion," displayed across six galleries. The museum serves as a vital center for preserving, researching, and exhibiting Dunhuang’s rich cultural heritage.
